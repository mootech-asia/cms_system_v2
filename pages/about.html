Self-assessment

1. Do you play to escape from a boring or unhappy life?
2. After losing, do you feel the urge to win back what you lost as soon as possible?
3. When you play, do you tend to continue until all available funds are exhausted?
4. Have you ever lied to conceal the amount of money or time spent on gambling?
5. Have you lost interest in family, friends, or hobbies because of gambling?
6. When you run out of money while playing, have you felt despair or disappointment and wanted to play again quickly?

Do you feel depressed or suicidal due to gambling? If most of your answers are "Yes", you may be experiencing gambling addiction. For free and independent advice, please contact organizations such as Gambling Therapy (https://www.gamblingtherapy.org/) or contact us.

Bet management

While most people gamble recreationally, for some it can become a problem.
You may find it helpful to remember:
1. Gambling should be fun and not seen as a way to make money.
2. Only gamble within your financial means.
3. Keep track of the time and money you spend on gambling.
4. Consider installing gambling site blocking software such as Betfilter (www.betfilter.com) or Gamblock (www.gamblock.com) on your mobile or tablet.
5. If you want to talk to someone about problem gambling, contact us or one of the organizations mentioned above.
